Intro to

Yoga & Mobility

Welcome to the Yoga and Mobility section! What is yoga? Yoga is a Hindu spiritual and ascetic discipline, including breath control, simple meditation and the adoption of specific bodily postures. It is widely practised for health and relaxation. In this section, you will learn about the basic equipments of yoga, the benefits and the components of it.

Benefits of Yoga

Helps Manage Stress

Yoga encourages you to turn inwards and create awareness of the outside factors that cause you stress. When you practise regularly, you'll start to understand how the breathing techniques, stillness and heat of the room help your body and mind relax.

Boosts Concentration, Focus & Memory

Do you find it hard to focus on certain daily tasks? Then giving yoga a try could help you regain the ability to get things done efficiently without getting distracted! Yoga can definitely boost concentration and memory because you have to focus during your practice.

Self-Care

In a fast-moving world, people not only leave behind their morals and their loved ones, but they also forget about themselves. Yoga makes a person meet him or herself again. It makes a person remember that self-care is as important as caring about your most beloved thing in life.

8 simple yoga poses for relaxation







Pose #1: Child's Pose (Balasana)

This calming pose is a good default pause position. You can use child’s pose to rest and refocus before continuing to your next pose. It gently stretches your lower back, hips, thighs, knees and ankles and relaxes your spine, shoulders and neck.

How to do it:
Seat on your heels, the tops of your feet pressing into the mat or floor, and pressing your chest toward your thighs. Then, extend your arms forward, with your palms facing either upwards or downwards.




Pose #2: Corpse Pose (Savasana)

Move into the ultimate state of relaxation in Corpse Pose. This pose allows you to let go, easing the physical tension and mental stress you’re holding onto throughout your entire body. Normally, yoga practices will end with this pose.

How to do it:
Separate your legs apart by hip distance and release any tension in your body. But don't forget to face your palms upwards. Just relax your whole body and let your breathing occur naturally.




Pose #3: Legs-Up-The-Wall Pose (Viparita Karani)

This pose is one of the greatest poses for settling the nervous system. In other words, it can move you from fight-or-flight to rest-and-digest, releasing tension and helping you to unwind. Keen to have a peaceful night of sleep? Then don't miss it!

How to do it:
Raise your legs straight, resting against the wall at right angle to the torso. Tuck your tailbone as close to a wall as you can and rest your back and head on the ground with your arms relaxed.




Pose #4: Downward-Facing Dog Pose (Adho Mukha Svanasana)

One of yoga's most widely known pose, this pose lengthens and stretches the posterior chain of the body, including the calves, hamstrings, glutes and back. It is an inversion as the head is below the heart, which can have a calming effect.

How to do it:
Get down on all fours. Start on the floor with your shoulders above your wrists. Then, lift your knees and tuck your toes against the mat or ground. Your body should now resemble an upside-down “V” shape.




Pose #5: Seated Forward Bend Pose (Paschimottanasana)

Paschimottanasana promotes relaxation throughout your entire body, from calming your brain to releasing tension in your legs. You’ll find that physical and mental stress are released when you move into this pose.

How to do it:
Stretch your legs forward and bring your arms up over your head. Inhale to lengthen your spine, and exhale to hinge forward slightly. When you are ready, take hold of your ankles or shins, whichever you can reach.




Pose #6: Melting Heart Pose (Anahatasana)

A slightly challenging yet effective pose, Anahatasana is known as a heart-opening yoga pose that energizes the whole body. It offers amazing mental benefits, including developing compassion, elevating your mood and helping to achieve a sense of inner calm.

How to do it:
From all fours, knees a little wider than hip-distance apart, lower down onto your elbows. Then straighten your arms in front of you and place your chin or forehead on the floor, allowing yourself to soften in the pose.




Pose #7: Tree Pose (Vrksasana)

For many of us, feelings of stress causes us to feel weak and unstable in both body and mind. Standing balancing postures like this one are a very tangible way to reconnect to your roots, find your stable center, and grow a feeling of power and strength.

How to do it:
In standing position, lift your left foot, bend your left knee and bring the sole of your left foot high onto your inner right thigh. Focus your gaze on something that doesn't move to help you keep your balance.




Pose #8: Bridge Pose (Sarvangasana)

The Bridge Pose is an inverted back-bending asana in hatha yoga and modern yoga as exercise. It stretches our chest, neck, spine, and hips and also calms the brain and central nervous system effectively, which helps in alleviating stress and also mild depression.

How to do it:
Lie on your back with your knees bent, your legs and feet parallel. Move your feet closer to your buttocks. Press down firmly through both of your feet and inhale to raise your hips, lifting it from the pubic bone.
