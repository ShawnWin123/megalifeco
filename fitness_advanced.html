Intro to

The Advanced Exercise

Welcome to the Advanced Exercise section! Congratulation to you if you are able to finish Beginner and Intermediate Journey. In this section, we will be learning 5 advanced skills like Handstand Push Up, Front Lever, Straddle Planche, Human Flag and muscle up. You will use the strength that you train in the previous section such as push and pull. In order to start, please select a skill to learn.








Before We Start

Time Spent

This section will cost you 1-3 years to complete it as it is the most challenging section. During workout, you need do the workout for sets and reps. We will recommend you to do the whole workout for 3 sets each and rest for 3 minutes between each workout.

Recovery Time

Arrangement of workout time is very important for building strength and muscle as the muscle needs enough rest to recover from heavy exercise. We recommend you to do 5 workout sessions per week, but don't it in a row. Do treat your muscle well!

Safety Control

You will learn many skills that are very cool but dangerous, so we will recommened you to find a partner to keep you safe. Also, don't try to do some of the exercise if you haven't reach that strength. And the most important thing is to warm up to prevent you from injuries.

Advanced Skills #1 - Human Flag

The Human Flag is a feat of strength where the body is parallel to the ground supported by a vertical bar. A straight line is formed using the arms and body.




Progression 1: Hanging side pull

Dead hang on a pull-up bar. Depress and retract your shoulders. Depress deeper on one shoulder and shift the weight of your lower body to that side. Maintain the position for a specified time. Do the same for the other side.



Progression 2: 45 degree hanging hold

Hang on a support beam with one arm then hold a vertical bar with the other. Push the arm holding on a vertical bar. Engage your obliques and move your weight sidewards until your body is in a straight line at a 45-degree angle. Hold the position for a specified time.



Progression 3: Flag plank

Hold the vertical pole with your top hand and the other hand on the ground or a bar on the floor. Straighten both of your arms and extend your legs while feet together. Hold the position for a specified time.



Progression 4: Straddle Flag Lower Down

Grip the pole to begin in the starting posture. Lift your whole body straight up jumping into a vertical position hold. From there, straddle your legs and slowly lower down your body to the floor while maintaining proper flag form. Repeat the process for reps.



Progression 5: Double Leg Drops

Grip the pole to begin in the starting position. Grip the pole to begin in the starting position. From there, slowly lower down your body to the floor while maintaining proper human flag form.



Final Progression:

Prepare hand placement on your equipment. Begin pushing and pulling with the respective upper torso musculature. Lift your lower torso until parallel to the ground. Hold for a specified time.

Advanced Skills #2 - Handstand Push Up

The Handstand Push Up is a dynamic bodyweight exercise which allows you to develop excellent upper body strength as well as balance and body control.




Progression 1: Frog Stand

Place your hands on the floor and spread your fingers wide. Your hands should be in line with your shoulders. Bring your knees on the outside of your elbows, bent your elbows, look towards the floor, and raise your feet off the ground.



Progression 2: Chest to Wall Handstand

Assume the starting position. Get into a plank position and use your hands, not your forearms, to rest. Lean the soles of your feet against the wall. Steadily walk your feet up the wall, keep pushing through the shoulders to keep your arms straight. Bring your hands as close to the wall as you comfortably can, keeping them a little over shoulder-width apart.



Progression 3: Wall HSPU

If you have finish the our Intermediate Exercise Course, you already have the strength to do HSPU. First, perform a Chest to Wall Handstand and keep your core enngage. Then, slowly learn forward and do a HSPU like how you do the Pike Push Up. Try to do for a few reps.



Progression 4: Free Handstand

Kick up into a handstand position by starting in a downward facing dog pose and quickly lifting one leg into the air while keeping your core and leg muscles engaged. As you lift your leg, shift your weight onto your hands and straighten your arms to support your body weight in the handstand position.



Progression 5: Assisted HSPU

Perform a HSPU with one friend gently holding your hand. This is to let you stay upside down longer and to prevent injuries.



Final Progression:

Here you go, kick up to handstand position and start doing push up!

Advanced Skills #3 - Straddle Planche

Straddle Planche is a strength-based full-body skill move that originated from gymnastics. Oftentimes, it leaves people in awe with its gravity-defying aesthetic.




Progression 1: Tuck Planche

It’s basically a planche with your knees tucked close to your chest to lessen the lever. It might not seem much, but it is still very challenging and is quite a remarkable achievement as well.



Progression 2: Advanced Tuck Planche

You might notice that the main difference is the different positions of the legs. The very slight adjustment of the tucked knees will add so much weight to your lower body. The key here is to lean forward enough so you can balance the position and rely more on your upper torso.



Progression 3: Frog Planche

This will you get a better feel of the planche since you’re pretty much near the straddle planche at this point. Knees are still bent and tucked close to the chest but hips are in an open position which adds more loading on your core.



Progression 4: Piked Straddle Planche

Legs are now extended and straight, but hips are piked similar to the advanced tuck planche. It’s best to practice this hold on parallettes instead of the floor to make room for the extended legs.



Final Progression:

Lean forward even further as the hips extend backward to achieve the straddle planche position. Aim to get your whole body parallel to the ground. The straddle leg position keeps the weight outwards requiring you less of the lean compared to a full planche.

Advanced Skills #4 - Front Lever

Front Lever is an advanced exercise that pull your full body weight to hold it in a straight line parallel to the ground on gymnastics rings or bar. That’s an insane level of strength for an exercise! Raising your feet off the floor by pulling your back is an amazing achievement enough!




Progression 1: Hanging Leg Raises

Grab on a bar and extend your legs so that they’re completely straight and hold the insides of your feet together. Then, tighten your core and lift you legs to a 90 degree from you stomach. Make sure to keep your knees straight and don't just drop down your legs after doing them.



Progression 2: Tuck Front Lever Hold

From the inverted hang, pull your body into a tucked position with your knees pulled in towards you ches t. You want your body to get into as much of a ball position as possible while keeping your arms straight.



Progression 3: Advanced Tuck Front Lever Hold

Lever Once you’ve got the tuck front lever down, you can level it up with the advanced tuck lever. To do it, get into the tuck front lever position and slowly release your knees from your chest. Keep your legs tucked up but release your knees slightly to work your abs more.



Progression 4: Single Leg Front Lever

With the single-leg front levers, you’re going to start extending your legs out like you would for a full front lever. However, you’re just gong to do it with one leg of a time. To start, get into the advanced tuck lever position with your back as flat as possible.



Progression 5: Straddle Front Lever

Now, you’re going to extend both of your legs out parallel to the ground. Get into the advanced tuck lever position, extend one leg out parallel to the ground, and then the other. Keep a gap in between your legs as if you were in a straddle position on the floor.



Final Progression: Full Front Lever

You’ve made it to the front lever. The goal here is to create a straight line from your head to your toes and hold the position for as long as possible without breaking form. Grab on to the bar and get yourself into a tuck-front position.

Advanced Skills #5 - Muscle Up

Muscle Up is an advanced strength training exercise, within the domain of calisthenics. It is a combinatio n routine of a radial pull-up followed by a dip.




Progression 1: Straight Bar Dip

A really good for building explosiveness of the muscle-up. Get on top of the bar and perform the exercise as deep as possible, try to hit the bar with your chest. This is important because you have the same range of motion with the Muscle-up.



Progression 2: Explosive Pull Up

The main goal for the exercise is to hit the bar with your chest. This is a pure strength exercise. When you’re training strength you don’t want to do too many repetitions. Never do more than 4 explosive Pull-ups, but try to do higher Pull-ups instead.



Progression 3: Assisted Muscle Up

You can trained with a resistance band or just do a few Jump Muscle Up. When you do a Muscle-up with a resistance band for the first time you want to make use of the momentum. You don’t pull up like a regular pull-up but until your waistline. In combination with the momentum, you will be able to get over the bar.



Progression 4: Kipping Muscle Up

If you still can't do it, this is the last progression for you. Grab on a bar and initiate swing with shoulders. Next, drive hips towards the bar while in the hollow position. At the same time, push down on the bar and lean forward your torso. You also can try it with a resistance band.



Final Progression: Full Muscle Up

Congratulation if you achieve it, this is the end of this section. See you next time!
