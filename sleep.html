Importance Of Sleep

Improves Memory

Even though sleep gives your body the rest it needs, your mind is still hard at work. It’s actually processing and consolidating your memories from the day. If you don’t get enough sleep, who knows where those memories go. Or worse, your mind might actually create false memories.

Boosts Your Mood

Your mind needs some time to process your emotions when you are asleep so that they can recognize and react the right way. When you cut that short, you tend to have more negative emotional reactions and fewer positive ones. Chronic lack of sleep can also raise the chance of having a mood disorder.

Boosts Your Immune System

When your body gets the sleep it needs, your immune cells and proteins get the rest they need to fight off whatever comes their way. Ongoing lack of sleep changes the way your immune cells work. They may not attack as quickly, and you could get sick more often.

How to Get Quality Sleep







Tip #1: Be consistent

Go to bed at the same time each night and get up at the same time each morning, even during the weekends. Your body clock will adjust and soon you will fall asleep easily and wake up feeling well rested. Research has shown that irregular bedtime schedule is significantly associated with a decrease in average sleep time per day.




Tip #2: Optimize your bedroom environment

Many people believe that the bedroom environment and its setup are key factors in getting a good night’s sleep. Make sure your bedroom is quiet, dark, relaxing, and at a comfortable temperature. Numerous studies point out that external noise, often from traffic, can cause poor sleep and long-term health issues.




Tip #3: Avoid consuming large meals and caffeine before bedtime

Our body slows down its functions at night to prepare for sleep, but consuming large meals can make it harder to digest and result in not able to get to as restful a state as it would otherwise. The same goes with caffeine. In a study, it proved that consuming caffeine up to 6 hours before bed significantly worsened sleep quality.




Tip #4: Exercise

Exercise is one of the best science-backed ways to improve your sleep and health. In fact, it can enhance all aspects of sleep and has been used to reduce symptoms of insomnia. One study in older adults determined that exercise nearly halved the amount of time it took to fall asleep and provided 41 more minutes of sleep at night.




Tip #5: Propper napping

Taking a nap during the day makes us feel refreshed. While short power naps are beneficial, long or irregular napping during the day can negatively affect your sleep. Napping for a long time can confuse your internal clock, meaning that you may struggle to sleep at night. Therefore, it is best to remember that nap duration should be around 20-30 minutes in the afternoon only.
