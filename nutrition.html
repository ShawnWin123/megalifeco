The Food Pyramid

The Food Pyramid is a visual representation of how different foods and drinks contribute towards a healthy balanced diet. The Food Pyramid allows individuals the flexibility to choose foods and drinks from each shelf depending on their food preferences.







Carbs (6-11 serving)

Carbohydrates are one of three main nutrients found in foods and drinks. Your body breaks down carbohydrates into glucose. Glucose, or blood sugar, is the main source of energy for your body. Examples include corn, wheat, pasta, and rice.




Fruit & Vegetable (2-4 serving)

Fruit and vegetables are a good source of vitamins and minerals, including folate, vitamin C and potassium. They're an excellent source of dietary fibre, which can help to maintain a healthy gut and prevent constipation and other digestion problems. Examples include broccoli, tomatoes, avocado, blueberries, banana, cherries, lettuce, spinach and much more.





Protein (2-3 serving)

Proteins are essential nutrients for the human body. They are one of the building blocks of body tissue and can also serve as a fuel source. As a fuel, proteins provide as much energy density as carbohydrates. Examples include meat, poultry, fish, eggs, and dairy foods.




Fats & Sweet (---)

Fats, oils and sweets give us extra calories. These types of foods are not needed in our diet so if consumed, have them in small amounts and less often. Aside from weight gain and obesity, consuming too many fats and sweets can lead to cardiovascular diseases and impaired heart health. Examples include salad dressings, mayonnaise, fast foods. Sweets like candy, soft drinks, syrups, jams, and jellies are made mostly of sugars.
